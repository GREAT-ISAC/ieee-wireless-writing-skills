# Area: AI+Wireless

AI+wireless work must justify why AI is necessary for the wireless problem.

Specify:

- Wireless task: estimation, beam management, sensing, localization, scheduling, resource allocation, protocol adaptation, anomaly detection, or network automation.
- AI role: representation learning, prediction, control, compression, denoising, optimization surrogate, foundation model, or agentic network management.
- Inputs available at deployment: CSI, IQ, pilots, spectrum snapshots, logs, topology, traffic, sensor data, or side information.
- Baselines: classical communication/signal-processing methods, optimization heuristics, compact ML models, and current neural methods.
- Constraints: latency, memory, energy, feedback, pilot overhead, privacy, distribution shift, and online adaptation.

Do not present AI as novelty by itself. The novelty must be tied to a wireless assumption, constraint, representation, objective, or deployment boundary.
